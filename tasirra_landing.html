The Mission

We fix what
government won't.

TASIRRA is a national community-impact infrastructure initiative. We restore South Africa's roads, public spaces, and essential infrastructure through community funding, corporate partnerships, and full transparency.

Project 001 is the proof of concept: a road restoration in Bochabela, Mangaung, Bloemfontein. Self-funded. In progress. Documented in real time. We are building the system to repeat it everywhere.

The Founder

Built by someone
who refused to wait.

Siphosethu Dingani is 20 years old and studying to become a civil engineer. When he saw the road in Bochabela deteriorating with no government response, he did not file a complaint. He funded the restoration himself and got to work.

That project, started without asking a single person for money, is the proof that this model works. TASIRRA is the system built to scale that action across South Africa.

How It Works

Simple. Transparent.
Accountable.

Every rand is tied to a specific project. Every project is documented from start to finish. Every result is visible to every member.

01

Community Funds

Members contribute monthly. Corporate partners donate machinery, materials, or expertise. Every project is pre-scoped — you know exactly what your money builds before you commit.

02

TASIRRA Builds

We coordinate contractors, construction partners, and community volunteers on the ground. Real-time updates, photos, and progress reports throughout every project.

03

You See the Result

Before. After. Numbers. A physical result in a real South African community, documented permanently. Not a PDF report — a road, a school wall, a public space.
